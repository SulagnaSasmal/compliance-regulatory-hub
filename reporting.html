Regulatory Reporting

SAR filing standards, CTR procedures, FinCEN timelines, quality standards, and voluntary disclosure protocols.
Filing Obligations
All BSA reports must be filed electronically through the FinCEN BSA E-Filing System. Paper filings are not accepted. Failure to file a required SAR or CTR is a federal violation subject to civil penalties up to $57,317 per day for negligent violations and up to $1 million per day for willful violations.

Tipping Off Prohibition
It is unlawful to notify any person named in, or the subject of, a SAR that the report has been filed or is being considered (31 USC 5318(g)(2)). This includes indirect disclosure through email, phone, or actions that suggest investigation. Violation is a federal criminal offense.

SAR Quality Standards

FinCEN evaluates SAR quality based on content completeness and narrative clarity. All SARs must:

  • Answer the "5 W's": Who, What, When, Where, and Why the activity is suspicious
  • Use clear, chronological narrative — avoid jargon and acronyms without definition
  • Identify all subjects with complete identifying information where available
  • Specify exact dollar amounts and transaction dates
  • Describe the typology (e.g., structuring, rapid movement, layering) being reported
  • Reference relevant prior SARs on the same subject where applicable
  • Avoid legal conclusions (do not characterize as "money laundering" — describe the facts)

Currency Transaction Report (CTR)

Filing Requirements Overview

A CTR must be filed for each transaction (or series of related transactions) involving more than $10,000 in currency (cash) conducted by, through, or to a financial institution in a single business day. Key rules:

  • Multiple transactions by the same customer on the same business day must be aggregated
  • Transactions conducted "on behalf of" another person must be attributed to that person
  • Both deposits and withdrawals meeting the threshold require filing
  • Currency exchanges, coin purchases, and redemptions of monetary instruments also qualify

CTR Operational Process

  1. Detection — Teller system or back-office system flags qualifying cash transaction at end of business day
  2. Review — Compliance reviews aggregation accuracy and exemption status
  3. Data Collection — Collect all required fields (subject info, transaction info, account info)
  4. OFAC Screening — Screen CTR subject against OFAC before filing
  5. Filing — File via FinCEN BSA E-Filing System by 11:59 PM (Eastern) on the 15th calendar day after the transaction date
  6. Retention — Store copy in BSA recordkeeping system for 5 years
CTR Exemptions
Eligible customers may be exempted from CTR filing using FinCEN Form 110 (Designation of Exempt Person / DOEP). Two exemption phases are available. Phase I exempt entities (e.g., banks, publicly traded companies) require no annual renewal. Phase II exempt entities require biennial renewal and annual independent audit of the exemption program.

Quality Assurance & Reporting Metrics

The Compliance function must track and report the following metrics to the BSA Officer and Board on a quarterly basis:

  • Total SARs filed (by typology, business line, geography)
  • SAR filing timeliness rate (% filed within required deadline)
  • SAR void / amendment rate (indicator of initial quality)
  • Total CTRs filed and CTR exemptions in use
  • Average alert-to-SAR cycle time
  • Alerts closed without SAR — L1, L2, L3 disposition breakdown
  • Law enforcement requests received and response completion rate
Law Enforcement Requests
Institutions may share SAR information with law enforcement upon a formal legal process (subpoena, court order, or National Security Letter). FinCEN maintains a 314(b) program allowing voluntary information sharing between financial institutions to identify potential money laundering. Enroll in 314(b) to enable collaborative investigation.
