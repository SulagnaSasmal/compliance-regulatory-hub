OFAC Sanctions Screening

SDN list obligations, sanctions programs, blocking and rejecting transactions, OFAC reporting, and hit disposition procedures.
Zero-Tolerance Obligation
OFAC sanctions compliance is strict liability — there is no knowledge requirement. Any U.S. person (including U.S. financial institutions and their foreign branches) that facilitates a transaction involving a sanctioned party or jurisdiction may face civil penalties regardless of intent. Civil penalties can reach the greater of $356,579 per violation or twice the transaction value.

OFAC Overview

The Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC), a division of the U.S. Department of the Treasury, administers and enforces economic and trade sanctions. OFAC sanctions programs are based on U.S. foreign policy and national security objectives and target:

  • Foreign countries and regimes
  • Terrorists and terrorist organizations
  • International narcotics traffickers
  • Proliferators of weapons of mass destruction
  • Other threats to U.S. national security, foreign policy, or economy

Screening Scope & Frequency

OFAC screening must be applied across the following touchpoints:

  • Customer Onboarding — Full name, aliases, date of birth, and address screened against all OFAC lists
  • Periodic Re-screening — Entire customer base re-screened within 24 hours of each OFAC list update
  • Transaction Screening — Real-time screening of all payment originators, beneficiaries, intermediaries, and referenced parties
  • Counterparty Screening — Third-party vendors, agents, and business partners screened at onboarding and annually
  • Wire / ACH Screening — All fields in payment messages screened (name, address, account number, reference information)
Fuzzy Matching Requirements
Automated screening systems must use fuzzy matching algorithms capable of detecting name variations, transliterations, nicknames, and partial matches. A minimum 85% name match score is required for alert generation. False negative rates must be validated during annual system testing.

Annual Reporting Requirement
Institutions holding blocked property must file an Annual Report of Blocked Property with OFAC by September 30 each year covering all property blocked during the prior calendar year. Failure to report is itself a sanctions violation.

OFAC License Types

Certain otherwise-prohibited transactions may be authorized through an OFAC license:

  • General License — Publicly available; permits specific categories of transactions without requiring individual application (e.g., GL-13 for Iran humanitarian goods)
  • Specific License — Requires application to OFAC; issued on a case-by-case basis for transactions not covered by a general license

All licensed transactions must be documented with the applicable license number and retained for 5 years.

OFAC Voluntary Disclosure
OFAC's Enforcement Guidelines provide for significant penalty mitigation (typically 50% or greater reduction) where an institution identifies a potential violation and submits a timely, good-faith Voluntary Self-Disclosure (VSD). Contact the BSA Officer and Legal immediately upon discovery of a potential OFAC violation.
