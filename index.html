Overview & Compliance Framework

Enterprise reference for regulatory obligations, program structure, and compliance operations.
Scope
This documentation applies to all regulated financial products and services. All personnel with compliance obligations must adhere to the policies described herein. Regulatory requirements referenced reflect U.S. federal standards as of the document revision date.

Program Overview

The Compliance & Regulatory Hub provides centralized reference documentation for the organization's financial crime compliance program. It covers applicable U.S. federal regulations, internal program controls, operational procedures, and governance requirements.

The compliance program is structured around four pillars: Know Your Customer (KYC), Anti-Money Laundering (AML), Sanctions Screening, and Regulatory Reporting. Each pillar has dedicated policies, procedures, and system controls described in the sections of this hub.

Compliance Program Components

A compliant BSA/AML program must include the following five pillars as required by FinCEN:

  1. Internal policies, procedures, and controls — Written policies governing AML, KYC, sanctions, and reporting obligations.
  2. Designation of a BSA/AML Compliance Officer — A qualified individual with authority and resources to administer the program.
  3. Ongoing employee training — Role-based training on AML typologies, red flags, escalation procedures, and regulatory requirements.
  4. Independent testing (audit) — Periodic independent review of the compliance program's effectiveness.
  5. Customer Due Diligence (CDD) — Risk-based identification, verification, and ongoing monitoring of customers, including beneficial ownership.
Program Review Cadence
Compliance policies and procedures must be reviewed at minimum annually, or upon material regulatory changes, new product launches, or significant operational changes.

Risk-Based Approach

All compliance controls are calibrated using a risk-based approach (RBA) as required by FinCEN and endorsed by FATF. The organization maintains a written Enterprise Risk Assessment (ERA) that categorizes compliance risk across:

  • Products & Services — Payment rails, account types, digital channels
  • Customer Segments — Retail, commercial, MSBs, PEPs, high-risk industries
  • Geographies — High-risk jurisdictions, FATF grey/black list countries
  • Distribution Channels — Direct, agent, third-party, online

Risk ratings (Low / Medium / High / Prohibited) drive customer due diligence depth, monitoring thresholds, and transaction limits.
