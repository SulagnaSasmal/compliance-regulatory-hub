Customer Due Diligence (CDD / EDD)

Risk-based CDD tiers, Enhanced Due Diligence requirements, beneficial ownership standards, and ongoing monitoring.
Regulatory Basis
FinCEN's CDD Rule (effective May 11, 2018; 31 CFR 1010.230) requires covered financial institutions to collect and verify beneficial ownership for legal entity customers and to conduct ongoing customer due diligence. This rule added a fifth pillar to the BSA/AML compliance program.

CDD Risk Tiers

The CDD program applies three risk tiers. The tier determines the depth of due diligence required at onboarding and on an ongoing basis:

TierRisk LevelCriteriaDue Diligence Depth
Tier 1 Low Domestic retail customer, low transaction volume, FATF-compliant geography, no adverse media or PEP indicators Standard CDD — CIP only, periodic automated monitoring
Tier 2 Medium Commercial entity (<25 employees), self-employed customers, elevated geography, moderate transaction volume Standard CDD + beneficial ownership collection + annual relationship review
Tier 3 (EDD) High PEPs, MSBs, high-risk industries (cannabis, gambling, arms), FATF grey/black list countries, SAR history Enhanced Due Diligence — source of funds/wealth documentation, senior management approval, enhanced monitoring, annual in-person or documented review
Prohibited Prohibited Shell banks with no physical presence, OFAC SDN-designated entities, anonymous/bearer share structures Relationship not permitted — must be declined or terminated

Beneficial Ownership Requirements

For all legal entity customers (excluding sole proprietors and certain regulated entities), the organization must collect and verify beneficial ownership as follows:

Ownership Prong

Collect and verify identifying information for each individual who, directly or indirectly, owns 25% or more of the equity interests of a legal entity customer.

Control Prong

Collect and verify identifying information for a single individual with significant managerial control over the entity — typically the CEO, CFO, COO, President, or equivalent. This applies even where no individual owns 25% or more.

Certification Form
Beneficial ownership information must be collected using either the FinCEN Beneficial Ownership Certification Form or an equivalent institution-developed form. The certification must be signed by the person opening or managing the account.

Verification

Beneficial owners must be verified using the same documentary or non-documentary methods applied to individuals under the CIP. Beneficial owners who are also customers of the institution may be subject to expedited verification using existing records.

Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) — Procedures

EDD is mandatory for all Tier 3 customers and must include the following at minimum:

  1. Source of Funds (SOF) — Documentation of the origin of funds expected to flow through the account (e.g., payroll, business revenue, investment proceeds, loan proceeds).
  2. Source of Wealth (SOW) — For PEPs and high-net-worth individuals, documentation of how the customer's overall wealth was accumulated.
  3. Nature and Purpose of Relationship — Detailed written description of anticipated account activity, expected transaction types, volumes, and counterparty profiles.
  4. Senior Management Approval — Written approval from a Compliance Officer or above is required before onboarding any EDD customer.
  5. Ongoing Enhanced Monitoring — Real-time or near-real-time monitoring with lower alert thresholds than standard customers.
  6. Periodic Senior Review — Annual documented relationship review by Compliance staff, including updated SOF/SOW assessment.
PEP Definition
Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs) include current or former senior political figures (heads of state, cabinet ministers, senior judges, senior military officers, senior executives of state-owned enterprises), as well as their immediate family members and known close associates. Foreign PEPs automatically require EDD. Domestic PEPs are subject to risk-based EDD.

Adverse Media & Negative News Screening

All customers must be screened against adverse media databases at onboarding and on a rolling basis. Adverse media categories requiring escalation include:

  • Criminal charges or convictions (fraud, corruption, money laundering, drug trafficking, terrorism)
  • Regulatory sanctions, enforcement actions, or license revocations
  • Credible allegations from reputable journalistic outlets
  • Insolvency, bankruptcy, or significant financial distress indicating potential for financial crime
Documenting CDD Decisions
All CDD risk tier assignments, EDD determinations, and periodic review decisions must be documented with rationale in the customer's compliance record. Undocumented decisions are a primary examination finding during regulatory reviews.
