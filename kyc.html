Know Your Customer (KYC)

Customer Identification Program (CIP), identity verification requirements, and onboarding controls.
Regulatory Basis
KYC and CIP requirements are mandated under 31 USC 5318(l) and the implementing regulations at 31 CFR 1020.220 (banks) and 31 CFR 1023.220 (broker-dealers). Non-compliance may result in civil money penalties exceeding $1 million per violation.

Customer Identification Program (CIP)

All regulated entities must maintain a written Customer Identification Program proportionate to the size and nature of the business. The CIP must include procedures for:

  • Collecting identifying information from each customer prior to account opening
  • Verifying the identity of each customer within a reasonable time
  • Maintaining records of the information used for verification
  • Consulting government-provided lists for known or suspected terrorists
  • Providing notice to customers that information is being collected for identity verification

Identity Verification Methods

Documentary Verification

Acceptable primary identity documents include:

  • U.S. unexpired state-issued driver's license or ID card
  • U.S. passport or passport card
  • Foreign government-issued passport with biometric data page
  • Permanent Resident Card (Green Card)
  • Military ID (DoD CAC)

Non-Documentary Verification

When documentary verification is not feasible (e.g., online onboarding), the following non-documentary methods may be used:

  • Credit bureau file inquiry (Experian, Equifax, TransUnion)
  • Automated KYC/identity proofing platforms (NIST 800-63-3 IAL2+)
  • Biometric liveness detection combined with document OCR
  • Knowledge-based authentication (KBA) — secondary use only
  • Bank account micro-deposit verification
Digital Onboarding
Identity verification for digital onboarding channels must meet NIST SP 800-63-3 Identity Assurance Level 2 (IAL2) at minimum for accounts with transaction limits above $10,000/day. Liveness detection is required for all selfie-based verification flows.

Ongoing KYC Refresh Requirements

Customer KYC data must be refreshed on a schedule commensurate with risk rating:

Risk TierRefresh FrequencyTrigger Events
LowEvery 5 yearsAddress change, name change
MediumEvery 3 yearsOccupation change, transaction pattern change
High / EDDEvery 12 monthsSAR filing, AML alert escalation, PEP status change
KYC Failures & Account Blocking
Accounts that fail to provide updated KYC documentation within 30 days of a refresh request must be transactionally restricted. Accounts pending more than 60 days without resolution must be escalated to the BSA Officer for potential termination.

Record Retention

Under 31 CFR 1020.220(a)(3), CIP records must be retained for a minimum of 5 years after the date the account is closed, or 5 years after the record is made, whichever is later. Records include:

  • All identifying information collected
  • A description of the verification method and results
  • Resolution of any identity discrepancies
  • Copies of identification documents (where permissible)
