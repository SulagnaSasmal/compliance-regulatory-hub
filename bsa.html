Bank Secrecy Act (BSA)

BSA program requirements, recordkeeping obligations, reporting thresholds, and FinCEN filings.
Primary Statute
The Bank Secrecy Act (31 USC 5311–5336), also referred to as the Currency and Financial Transactions Reporting Act, is the cornerstone of U.S. financial crime compliance. Implementing regulations are found at 31 CFR Chapter X (FinCEN).

BSA Program Requirements

Every covered financial institution must implement and maintain a BSA compliance program satisfying the "five pillars" of an adequate program under 31 CFR 1020.210:

PillarRequirementOwner
1. Policies & ProceduresWritten BSA/AML policies approved by the Board of Directors annuallyBSA Officer / Legal
2. BSA Compliance OfficerDesignated qualified officer with full authority and adequate resourcesSenior Management
3. TrainingAnnual AML training for all applicable personnel; job-specific training for compliance staffBSA Officer / HR
4. Independent AuditPeriodic independent BSA program audit (typically annual for banks, risk-based for MSBs)Internal Audit / External Auditor
5. Customer Due DiligenceRisk-based CDD including beneficial ownership collection and verificationBSA Officer / Operations

Currency Transaction Report (CTR) — Operational Detail

Aggregation Requirements

Multiple currency transactions throughout a single business day by or on behalf of the same customer must be aggregated when the total exceeds $10,000. Aggregation applies to:

  • Multiple deposits at different branches by the same customer
  • Deposits by third parties on behalf of a customer (known as "on behalf of" transactions)
  • Transactions conducted at an ATM or teller combined on the same day
Structuring Warning
Intentionally breaking up transactions to avoid the $10,000 CTR threshold (structuring) is a federal crime under 31 USC 5324, regardless of whether the underlying funds are from legitimate sources. Employees must not advise customers on structuring or facilitate structured transactions.

CTR Exemptions

The following entities may be exempt from CTR requirements after proper designation:

  • Phase I Exempt: Banks, government entities, publicly traded companies (NYSE, NASDAQ, or Tier 1 foreign exchange)
  • Phase II Exempt: Eligible non-listed businesses and payroll customers meeting specific criteria (established customer, operating business, ≥5 deposits/year within 12-month period)

FinCEN Registration — MSBs

Money Services Businesses (MSBs) must register with FinCEN within 180 days of establishment and renew every two years. MSB registration is required for:

  • Money transmitters (including payment processors, digital asset businesses)
  • Currency dealers and exchangers (exceeding $1,000/day for any one person)
  • Check cashers (exceeding $1,000/day for any one person)
  • Issuers / sellers / redeemers of money orders (exceeding $1,000/day for any one person)
  • Prepaid access programs and sellers
BSA Officer Authority
The BSA Officer must have direct access to the Board of Directors and must not be subordinate to any business line officer whose activities the BSA program is designed to oversee. This independence requirement is examined by regulators at every BSA examination.
